Robert Sapolsky's book "Behave: The Biology of Humans at Our Best and Worst" is a comprehensive examination of the biological and environmental factors that shape human behavior. Sapolsky argues that human behavior is the result of a complex interplay between genes, hormones, brain structures, and social and cultural factors.

He explores topics such as aggression, morality, empathy, addiction, and mental illness, and offers insights into the ways in which these behaviors are influenced by factors such as childhood experiences, socialization, and environmental stressors.

Sapolsky also delves into the neuroscience of decision-making, the role of free will in behavior, and the implications of his research for understanding and addressing social issues such as poverty, inequality, and violence.

In Summary

Overall, "Behave" is a compelling and thought-provoking exploration of the many factors that contribute to human behavior, and offers insights into how we might better understand and shape our own actions and the actions of others.
